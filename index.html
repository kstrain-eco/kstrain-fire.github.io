Current Research

My research interests lie at the intersection of invasion ecology, disturbance ecology, and conservation biology. I am interested in understanding how wildfire, elevational gradients, and climate change affect plant community assembly within an ecosystem.

Plant community and ecosystem responses to fire and plant invasion

As larger and more frequent fires continue to shape the landscape in the western United States and Great Masin, understanding the effect of fire on ecosystem processes and plant community assemply becomes increasingly important. We are currently monitoring post-fire recovery of vegetation in Lamoille Canyon near Elko, NV, to better understand the effect of burn presecnce and severity, time since fire, and herbaceous species diveristy on soil microbial activity and nutrient dynamics.

Community effects of a non-native grass in East-Central Texas

We surveyed savannas and grasslands in fifteen privately owned properties across six counties east of Austin, Texas. Paspalum notatum, a range improvement grass introduced from South america was the most common non-native species, despite differences among sites in soils and woody cover. P. notatum was found to be associated with low vasucalar species richness. Our findings indicate that P. notatum should be classified as an invasive species in this region.

Use of a consumer-grade UAV for Ashe juniper (Juniperus ashei) managment

A history of fire suppression and overgrazing in Texas has caused conditions favorable for Ashe juniper Juniperus ashei to colonize previously open areas, thus decreasing the forage value of the land. For Texas landowners interested in preventing woody encroachment, managment of Ashe juniper can be costly. We developed a model to demonstrate how how land owners, land managers and citizen scientists may use consumer grade unmanned aircraft systems (UAS), publicly available TNRIS aerial images, and cloud storage to develop a personal geographic information system. Our project resulted in an affordable tool to support ranch operations, wildlife reserves, agricultural facilities, or individual home ownership.
